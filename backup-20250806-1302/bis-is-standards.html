Understanding BIS and Indian Standards

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is the national standardization body of India, responsible for establishing and maintaining standards across industries. BIS develops Indian Standards (IS) that specify technical requirements for products, processes, and services to ensure quality, safety, and reliability.

Regulatory Authority

BIS is the sole national standards body with authority to formulate standards. IS codes are legally binding for many safety-critical applications including signage.

Quality Assurance

IS standards ensure consistent quality, performance, and safety across all signage manufacturers and installations throughout India.

International Alignment

Indian Standards align with international best practices (ISO, IEC standards) while considering local conditions and requirements.

IS 9457: Fire Exit Signs Specification

IS 9457 is the definitive Indian Standard for fire exit signage, establishing comprehensive requirements for design, dimensions, colors, materials, and installation of emergency exit signs.

Design Specifications

Dimensional Requirements:

  • Standard Size: Minimum 300mm x 150mm
  • Large Format: 450mm x 225mm for high ceilings
  • Letter Height: Minimum 25mm for text
  • Pictogram Size: 100mm x 100mm minimum
  • Border Width: 5mm minimum around sign

Shape and Orientation:

  • Rectangular format with 2:1 aspect ratio
  • Horizontal orientation for wall mounting
  • Vertical orientation for ceiling suspension

Color Specifications

Primary Colors:

  • Background: Green - Pantone 347 C
  • Text/Symbols: White - Pantone Bright White
  • RGB Values: Green (0, 122, 51), White (255, 255, 255)

Color Performance:

  • UV fade resistance for 10+ years
  • Color consistency across batches (ΔE < 2.0)
  • Non-reflective matte finish
  • High contrast ratio (minimum 7:1)

Typography Standards

Font Requirements:

  • Font Family: Sans-serif (Helvetica, Arial equivalent)
  • Weight: Medium to Bold (500-700)
  • Character Height: 25mm minimum
  • Stroke Width: 1/8 of character height

Text Layout:

  • "EXIT" in English (mandatory)
  • Regional language equivalent (where required)
  • Center-aligned text
  • Adequate spacing between characters

Material Specifications

Substrate Materials:

  • Acrylic: 3-5mm thickness, UV stabilized
  • Aluminum: 1.6mm thickness, powder coated
  • Self-adhesive Vinyl: Indoor/outdoor grade

Durability Requirements:

  • Minimum 10-year outdoor life
  • Fire resistance rating (Class 0 or A2)
  • Impact resistance (IK07 rating)
  • Temperature stability (-20°C to +70°C)

Pictogram Requirements

  • ISO 3864-1 compliant symbols
  • Running man figure with arrow
  • Door outline where applicable
  • Minimum 15mm line width
  • Clear contrast against background

Installation Standards

  • 2.0-2.4m mounting height
  • Perpendicular to viewing angle
  • Unobstructed viewing distance
  • Secure mounting system
  • Weather protection for outdoor use

Illumination Requirements

  • Minimum 50 lux on sign face
  • Emergency power backup (3 hours)
  • LED or fluorescent illumination
  • Even light distribution
  • Automatic failure detection

IS 12349: Safety Colors and Signs

IS 12349 establishes the color coding system for safety signage in India, defining specific colors for different types of safety messages and their applications across various industries.

Red - Prohibition & Fire

Applications:

  • Fire fighting equipment
  • Prohibition signs (No smoking, No entry)
  • Danger and warning signs
  • Emergency stop controls

Technical Specs: Pantone 18-1664 TPX, RGB (229, 62, 62)

Yellow - Warning & Caution

Applications:

  • Caution and warning signs
  • Hazard identification
  • Traffic control signage
  • Construction zone warnings

Technical Specs: Pantone 116 C, RGB (255, 221, 0)

Green - Safety & Emergency

Applications:

  • Emergency exits and escape routes
  • First aid equipment
  • Safety shower and eyewash stations
  • Assembly points

Technical Specs: Pantone 347 C, RGB (0, 122, 51)

Blue - Mandatory Actions

Applications:

  • Mandatory PPE requirements
  • Compulsory safety actions
  • Information and guidance signs
  • Safety equipment instructions

Technical Specs: Pantone 286 C, RGB (0, 114, 206)

Symbol Standards

Design Principles:

  • Simple, recognizable pictograms
  • Universal understanding (language-independent)
  • High contrast for visibility
  • Scalable vector graphics

Symbol Categories:

  • Prohibition symbols (circle with diagonal line)
  • Warning symbols (triangular border)
  • Mandatory symbols (circular blue background)
  • Emergency symbols (square or rectangular green)

Contrast and Visibility

Contrast Requirements:

  • Minimum 70% contrast ratio
  • Symbol to background contrast > 3:1
  • Text to background contrast > 4.5:1
  • Border definition for clarity

Viewing Distance:

  • Recognition distance = 100 x symbol height
  • Legibility distance = 200 x character height
  • Maximum viewing angle: ±45°

IS 15493: Photoluminescent Safety Signs

IS 15493 specifies requirements for photoluminescent safety signage that provide visibility during power failures without requiring external power sources.

Photoluminescent Properties

Luminance Requirements:

  • Initial Brightness: ≥ 150 mcd/m² after 10 minutes
  • Sustained Glow: ≥ 30 mcd/m² after 60 minutes
  • Long Duration: ≥ 3 mcd/m² after 10 hours
  • Charging Time: 15 minutes under 200 lux

Performance Standards:

  • Grade A materials for critical applications
  • Grade B materials for general use
  • Minimum 10-year performance guarantee
  • Non-radioactive materials only

Material Specifications

Substrate Requirements:

  • Photoluminescent Pigment: Strontium aluminate based
  • Binder System: UV-stable polymer matrix
  • Base Material: Aluminum, acrylic, or vinyl
  • Protective Coating: Anti-UV clear coat

Environmental Resistance:

  • Temperature range: -40°C to +80°C
  • Humidity resistance: up to 95% RH
  • UV stability: 2000+ hours QUV-B
  • Chemical resistance: Grade 2 minimum

Testing and Certification

Performance Tests:

  • Afterglow measurement (DIN 67510)
  • Photometric testing
  • Accelerated weathering tests
  • Adhesion and durability testing

Quality Assurance:

  • Batch testing requirements
  • Certification mark compliance
  • Traceability documentation
  • Regular factory audits

BIS Certification and Compliance

Understanding the certification process and compliance requirements for IS standard signage in India.

1

Design Verification

Ensure all design elements comply with relevant IS standards including dimensions, colors, materials, and symbols.

2

Material Testing

Submit samples for testing at BIS-approved laboratories for performance, durability, and safety compliance verification.

3

Manufacturing Audit

BIS conducts factory audits to ensure manufacturing processes meet quality standards and consistency requirements.

4

Certification Grant

Upon successful verification, BIS grants certification allowing use of ISI mark on compliant signage products.

ISI Mark Benefits

  • Legal compliance assurance
  • Quality guarantee for buyers
  • Market credibility and trust
  • Government contract eligibility

Ongoing Compliance

  • Regular surveillance audits
  • Batch testing requirements
  • Quality system maintenance
  • Corrective action protocols

Non-Compliance Risks

  • Legal penalties and fines
  • Market access restrictions
  • Liability in case of incidents
  • Reputation damage
